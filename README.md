##  Motivation

Traffic accidents remain one of the leading causes of injury and loss of life worldwide, posing a significant challenge for modern transportation systems. With the rapid advancement of autonomous driving technologies and intelligent transportation systems, understanding the underlying factors that contribute to accident occurrence has become increasingly critical.

As a student with a strong interest in robotics and autonomous systems, I am particularly motivated to explore how data-driven approaches can enhance safety in real-world environments. Autonomous vehicles rely heavily on perception, decision-making, and risk assessment modules, all of which depend on accurately understanding environmental and temporal conditions.

This project aims to bridge data science and autonomous systems by analyzing large-scale real-world traffic accident data and developing predictive models for accident risk. By identifying key contributing factors such as weather conditions, time of day, and road characteristics, the project seeks to provide insights that can support safer navigation strategies and more robust decision-making in intelligent systems.

Ultimately, this project reflects my broader goal of contributing to the development of safe, reliable, and intelligent autonomous systems through data-driven methodologies.





##  Data Resources

This project integrates multiple real-world datasets to enable a comprehensive and enriched analysis of traffic accident risk.

| Source | Data | Role |
|--------|------|------|
| US Accidents (Kaggle) | 7.7M records (2016–2023), including severity, GPS, weather and road conditions | Primary |
| Open-Meteo Historical API | Hourly historical weather data for sampled locations | Enrichment |
| NOAA Climate Normals | Regional monthly climate averages | Enrichment |
| OpenStreetMap (OSM) | Road network data including intersections and road types | Enrichment |
| US Census Data | Population density and regional characteristics | Enrichment |



##  Planned Analysis

The analysis will include the following steps:

- Data collection and preprocessing  
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)  
- Analysis of accident distribution across time, weather, and location  
- Feature engineering (temporal, environmental, and road-related features)  
- Identification of high-risk conditions  
- Development of predictive models for accident risk (e.g. Logistic Regression, Random Forest)  
- Evaluation of model performance using classification metrics (Accuracy, F1-score, ROC-AUC)
